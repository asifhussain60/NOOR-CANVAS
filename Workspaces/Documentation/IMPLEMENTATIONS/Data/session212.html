Imamat: The unbroken Chain of Guidance

The spiritual path is built upon a continuous chain of guidance. This sacred chain begins with Maulana Ali and Maulatina Fatima and extends through the Imams from their progeny. This is not a random historical sequence; it is a core part of Allah's divine plan for humanity, which reveals itself in different cycles or periods, known as a دور. Just as the world has its own distinct seasons, human history also has its own unique spiritual seasons. Each season is led by an Imam and the appointed Prophets of his time. You can also think of it as various chapters in a book. When the chapter of Prophet Nuh and his successors concluded, Allah began a new one by sending Prophet Ibrahim (AS), initiating a new era of guidance for the world, which was approximately 1000 years after Nuh (AS).

However, unlike Adam and Nuh (AS), Allah says something interesting about Ibrahim (AS). He honors him with the title of خلیل, which generally means "friend". While it is often translated as 'friend,' its meaning is much more profound. 

خلیل

The term Khalil (خليل) originates from the three-letter Arabic root خلل, meaning a gap. From this root, the word الخُلَّة emerges, representing the highest level of love and friendship—one that deeply occupies the heart and soul, leaving no خَلَل (gap) for others. A خليل is thus someone whose love has so thoroughly penetrated their heart that it becomes an integral part of their identity. This is why Ibrahim (AS) is called خليل الله (the intimate friend of Allah), as his heart was entirely filled with Allah's love. 

So Ibrahim (AS) attained this spiritual station by his absolute devotion, unwavering submission, and profound love for Allah. This unique relationship is highlighted in the Quran as:

Women (4:125)

‏ ‏وَمَنْ أَحْسَنُ دينًۭا مّمَّنْ أَسْلَمَ وَجْهَهُ للَّه وَهُوَ مُحْسنٌۭ وَٱتَّبَعَ ملَّةَ إبْرَهيمَ حَنيفًۭا وَٱتَّخَذَ ٱللَّهُ إبْرَهيمَ خَليلًۭا ‎  ‎

Translation:

And who is better in religion than one who submits himself to Allah while being a doer of good and follows the religion of Abraham, inclining toward truth? And Allah took Abraham as an intimate friend.

KALIMAAT: The Divine Words

Allah says in the Quran:

The Cow (2:124)

‏ ‏ وَإذ ٱبْتَلَىٰ إبْرَهمَ رَبُّهُ بكَلمَتٍۢ فَأَتَمَّهُنَّ ۖ قَالَ إنّى جَاعلُكَ للنَّاس إمَامًۭا ۖ قَالَ وَمن ذُرّيَّتى ۖ قَالَ لَا يَنَالُ عَهْدى ٱلظَّلمينَ ‎  ‎

Translation:

And [mention, O Muhammad], when Abraham was tried by his Lord with commands and he fulfilled them. [Allah] said, "Indeed, I will make you a leader for the people." [Abraham] said, "And of my descendants?" [Allah] said, "My covenant does not include the wrongdoers."  

The term "کلمات" appears frequently in the Quran. For example, in the story of Adam (AS), Allah says: فَتَلَقَّىٰٓ آدَمُ مِن رَّبِّهِۦ كَلِمَتٍۢ فَتَابَ عَلَيْهِ ۚ إِنَّهُۥ هُوَ ٱلتَّوَّابُ ٱلرَّحِيمُ - "Then Adam received from his Lord Words," and His Lord forgave him. Certainly, He is the Forgiving, the Merciful. Understanding the meaning of this term is therefore crucial.

What is a word?

A word is made up of a sequence of letters arranged in a specific order. Preserving this order is crucial for accurately conveying the word's intended meaning. In esoterics, each letter in a "word" represents a range of concepts—such as revelations, creations, identities, or, as with Ibrahim (AS), a series of tests. Ultimately, this process reveals deeper spiritual realities or, in the case of a word, unveils its ultimate meaning. In every case, the purpose of the words (کلمات) is to reveal the divine will of Allah(SWT). 

A Creative Force:

The word 'کلمۃ' means a word. Its plural is کلمات. It is used in several places in the Quran. The most famous example is Allah's creative act described as كُن فَيَكُونُ. 

Ya Sîn (36:82)

‏ ‏إنَّمَا أَمْرُهُ إذَا أَرَادَ شَيْـًٔا أَن يَقُولَ لَهُ كُن فَيَكُونُ ‎  ‎

Translation:

His command is only when He intends a thing that He says to it, "Be," and it is.  

Here, the word "كُن" ("Be") isn’t just another command expressed with letters. It’s the very essence of creation itself. It connects divine will with the physical world around us. So, a "كلمۃ" from Allah becomes a powerful act of divine energy, bringing realities into existence. When we see this idea in the context of spiritual truths, they aren’t just passive concepts—they are active, fundamental principles that form the foundation of the spiritual universe. 

A Living Being:

This shows that a کلمۃ can also be a living being, a perfect embodiment of Allah's will and command in the world. 

Expressions of an Inner Reality

From our own perspective, what is a word? A word is the outward expression of an inner thought or intention that cannot be seen. Before speaking, the idea exists only in our minds. When we say the word, we give that invisible idea a form that others can perceive.

Applying this metaphorically, higher spiritual realities exist in the spiritual realm, which is beyond our direct perception. A کلمۃ is the form that this divine reality takes to be understood by humans. It's the expression or manifestation of that eternal truth. For this reason, in تاویل, the Prophets and especially the Imams are considered the highest of the Kalimat. They're the living, human expressions of Allah's eternal guidance and knowledge on earth, making unseen spiritual realities known and accessible.

The "Speaking Word"

At the heart of our faith is a key principle. The physical Qur'an is referred to as the "Silent Book" (کتاب الصامت). Its letters and words are just ink on a page, unable to speak for themselves. To grasp its deepest meanings, we need the "Speaking Book" (کتاب الناطق). This was Maulana Ali during the time of the Prophet. Our Prophet (SWS) brought the silent Quran, which is unable to explain its hidden meanings. Therefore, he established Maulana Ali as his successor and the "Speaking Book" to explain the hidden meanings within the physical text. 

Similarly, every Imam of his period is also the "Speaking Word" of Allah. Quran says:

The Cow (2:1-3)

‏ ‏الم ‎ ‏ذَلكَ ٱلْكتَبُ لَا رَيْبَ فيه هُدًۭى لّلْمُتَّقينَ ‎ ‏ٱلَّذينَ يُؤْمنُونَ بٱلْغَيْب وَيُقيمُونَ ٱلصَّلَوٰةَ وَممَّا رَزَقْنَهُمْ يُنفقُونَ ‎  ‎

Translation:

Alif, Lam, Meem. This is the Book about which there is no doubt, a guidance for those conscious of Allah - Who believe in the unseen, establish prayer, and spend out of what We have provided for them,  

What is a book or a کتاب? It's a collection of words, organized in a specific way to convey meaning. Maulana Ali is the book, whose lineage contains the "words" representing the Imams, who are sent to this world to convey spiritual guidance. That's why the verse refers to "that book" rather than "this book". This verse is not referring to the physical book in our hands, but rather pointing to another entity: the divine book of guidance, which is Maulana Ali and the Imams in his progeny. They are the living embodiment of Allah's message. They are the speaking "word" that makes the "words" of the silent scripture relevant and understandable for every age. 

So, when the Qur'an mentions that Ibrahim (AS) was tested by certain "Words" (کلمات), it refers to the eternal principles of the Imamat that he had to recognize and embody. By doing so, he proved he was worthy of having a line of living, speaking, کلمات—the Imams—descend from him.

The Architect:

Think of a master architect's blueprint for a magnificent skyscraper.

  • The higher reality is the architect's complete vision—perfect, whole, but intangible.
  • The blueprint is the کلمۃ. The "word" or "command" translates the architect's intangible vision into a tangible, understandable form. It's not the building itself, but it contains all the information and authority needed to construct it perfectly. Without the blueprint, the vision remains locked away and inaccessible.

In the same way, the کلمات are the divine "blueprints" that express the will and knowledge of Allah. They are the commanding principles and, in their highest form, the living human guides who translate eternal spiritual truths into practical guidance for humanity. Therefore, "word" is the perfect term for a reality that is creative, commanding, expressive, and revelatory.

Why is Ibrahim (AS) a Divine Word?

Ibrahim (AS) fulfilled a series of profound promises and trials, described in the Qur'an as کلمات (divine words/commands). His perfect fulfillment of these trials demonstrated a level of submission and trust so complete that it earned him the unique and intimate title of خلیل اللہ, the intimate friend of Allah. 

The promise was not a single act but a lifelong covenant of absolute obedience, culminating in the most challenging test imaginable. On the outward, these Words represent the series of difficult commands and trials that Ibrahim (AS) had to undergo to prove his absolute submission to Allah. He had to leave his home and family, face persecution from his people, and finally, face the ultimate test of being commanded to sacrifice his beloved son, Ismail. By fulfilling these commands without hesitation, Ibrahim demonstrated the perfection of his submission.

On an inward (باطن) level, the "Words" represent the series of divine and spiritual realities, principles, and truths that Ibrahim (AS) had to recognize, embody, and master. In esoterics, these "Words" are connected to the spiritual ranks and the eternal creative command of Allah. They were not merely instructions for action, but rather tests of profound spiritual perception and revelation. Ibrahim's (AS) test was to recognize the spiritual hierarchy of the universe and to embody the virtues represented by these Divine Words. This demonstrated his capacity not just to be a Prophet who delivers a message, but an Imam (إمام) who can spiritually guide humanity.

An analogy for this is to think of a student being trained for a great responsibility. The outward "words" of the teacher are the assignments and tests in the curriculum. The student must complete these tasks. However, the actual inward reality of these tests is to determine if the student can truly understand and apply the deep principles the curriculum is designed to teach. Simply completing the assignments is not enough; the student must internalize the wisdom to become a master. Ibrahim's (AS) fulfillment of the "Words" was his internalization of divine truths, proving he was ready for the immense responsibility of Imamah (إمامة). This covenant Allah stated does not extend to the wrongdoers.

The Ultimate Test of Submission

His ultimate test of submission was Allah asking him to sacrifice his beloved son Ismail.
Those Who Set the Ranks (37:102)

‏ ‏فَلَمَّا بَلَغَ مَعَهُ ٱلسَّعْىَ قَالَ يَبُنَىَّ إنّى أَرَىٰ فى ٱلْمَنَام أَنّى أَذْبَحُكَ فَٱنظُرْ مَاذَا تَرَىٰ قَالَ يَأَبَت ٱفْعَلْ مَا تُؤْمَرُ ۖ سَتَجدُنى إن شَاءَ ٱللَّهُ منَ ٱلصَّبرينَ ‎  ‎

Translation:

And when he reached with him [the age of] exertion, he said, "O my son, indeed I have seen in a dream that I [must] sacrifice you, so see what you think." He said, "O my father, do as you are commanded. You will find me, if Allah wills, of the steadfast."  

Fulfilling this covenant is precisely what elevated him from the station of a prophet to the unique rank of خلیل (friend). A servant may obey out of duty, fear, or hope for reward. A friend, however, acts out of pure love, innate trust, and a deep, shared understanding. As I explained earlier, خلیل implies an intimacy where love and knowledge "permeate" the heart, leaving no room for doubt, ego, or self-interest. When Ibrahim (AS) was willing to sacrifice his son, he demonstrated that the love of Allah had permeated his entire being. His will became perfectly aligned with the Divine Will.

Therefore, he fulfilled the promise of total submission, both in its outward action and its inward spiritual reality. This perfect, all-encompassing fulfillment transformed his relationship with Allah into one of sacred friendship, a bond of unparalleled love and trust.

The Promise That Lives On

What did Allah do in return? Allah not just made Ibrahim an Imam, but also promised that the unbroken chain of Imamat and the chain of prophethood would continue in his lineage. Quran says:

The Cow (2:124)

‏ ‏ وَإذ ٱبْتَلَىٰ إبْرَهمَ رَبُّهُ بكَلمَتٍۢ فَأَتَمَّهُنَّ ۖ قَالَ إنّى جَاعلُكَ للنَّاس إمَامًۭا ۖ قَالَ وَمن ذُرّيَّتى ۖ قَالَ لَا يَنَالُ عَهْدى ٱلظَّلمينَ ‎  ‎

Translation:

And [mention, O Muhammad], when Abraham was tried by his Lord with commands and he fulfilled them. [Allah] said, "Indeed, I will make you a leader for the people." [Abraham] said, "And of my descendants?" [Allah] said, "My covenant does not include the wrongdoers."  

Since Ibrahim (AS) passed this ultimate test, Allah made him an Imam (a spiritual leader) for humanity and promised that this leadership, the Imamat, would continue forever in his family. By proving himself worthy, he laid the groundwork for the permanent institution of the امامۃ and نبوۃ. This divine promise ensured that humanity will always have a guide.

This sacred promise is a direct line from Ibrahim (AS) to the subsequent Prophets, leading to the final Prophet, Muhammad. Then to his successor, Maulana Ali, and then from him the chain of Imamat continues unbroken to the Imam of the present day. So, Ibrahim’s (AS) story is more than just a tale from the past. It's the foundation of the living principle of continuous guidance at the heart of our Sulemani faith. It's a powerful story of a trial passed, a promise fulfilled, and a divine gift—the light of the Imamat—that shines eternally.

If you examine this verse

قَالَ إِنِّى جَاعِلُكَ لِلنَّاسِ إِمَامًۭا قَالَ وَمِن ذُرِّيَّتِى قَالَ لَا يَنَالُ عَهْدِى ٱلظَّلِمِينَ

 [Allah] said, "Indeed, I will make you an Imam for the people." [Abraham] said, "And of my descendants?" [Allah] said, "My covenant does not include the wrongdoers." Now this doesn't make sense, right?  Allah says, 'I am planning to elevate you to the rank of an Imam!' He asks, "What about my lineage?" -meaning, does your promise apply to the chain of Imamat from my lineage? So how does Allah respond? My covenant does not include the wrongdoers! Why? Ismail and Ishaq (AS) are wrongdoers? Of course not! So then why did Allah say that?

This divine response transcended simply naming a specific individual, such as Ismail (اسماعيل) or Ishaq (اسحاق). Instead, Allah's statement, "My covenant does not include the wrongdoers," provided a profound and universal principle that governs the spiritual leadership (Imamate).

This response clarifies that while lineage is important, it is secondary to the spiritual purity and righteousness required for bearing the divine covenant. Allah's answer was not a denial of Prophet Ibrahim's (ابراهيم) descendants' potential for leadership, but a clear condition for it. It established that anyone from his progeny, whether Ismail (اسماعيل), Ishaq (اسحاق), or their descendants, who holds this sacred office will be free from wrongdoing.  Because if they ever did anything wrong, Allah's word would become false. It's incredible how the language contains the depths of profound meanings.

This highlights that the Imamate is a spiritual role rooted in infallible moral excellence and divine choice, ensuring that ongoing guidance—referred to as the "living word" and the "Rope of Allah" (حبل الله)—remains pure and uncontaminated for humanity. This is why Allah responded to Ibrahim's (ابراهيم) question by revealing the essential requirement (of عصمۃ) that everyone who inherits this high spiritual duty will possess - Infallibility.

The Soul's Journey to the Spiritual World

Certain chosen guides of Allah, such as Prophets, are granted the special ability to witness the spiritual world, known as the مَلَكُوت. This is not the physical world perceived by the senses, but a higher, unseen reality that is the origin of all existence. An analogy for this is to think of the physical world as a shadow and the مَلَكُوت as the real object casting that shadow. Ibrahim (AS) was one such Prophet who was shown this spiritual dominion over the heavens and the earth, allowing him to perceive a deeper layer of reality.

This profound spiritual experience was not unique to him. Other Prophets, such as Idris, Isa, and our Prophet, Muhammad (SWS), also experienced a spiritual ascent where their souls were elevated to a high station. 

  • For Idris (AS), Allah says: ‏وَرَفَعْنَهُ مَكَانًا عَلِيًّا - And we raised him to a high station. 
  • Similarly, Isa (AS) was also raised to Allah’s presence. ‏بَل رَّفَعَهُ ٱللَّهُ إِلَيْهِ ۚ وَكَانَ ٱللَّهُ عَزِيزًا حَكِيمًۭا ‏ - But Allah raised him up unto Himself. And Allah is Ever All­Powerful, All­Wise.
  • The most renowned of these spiritual journeys belongs to our Prophet SWS, called the Night Journey, the إِسْرَاء.
The Children of Israel (17:1)

‏ ‏سُبْحَنَ ٱلَّذى أَسْرَىٰ بعَبْده لَيْلًۭا مّنَ ٱلْمَسْجد ٱلْحَرَام إلَى ٱلْمَسْجد ٱلْأَقْصَا ٱلَّذى بَرَكْنَا حَوْلَهُ لنُريَهُ منْ آيَتنَا إنَّهُ هُوَ ٱلسَّميعُ ٱلْبَصيرُ ‎  ‎

Translation:

Exalted is He who took His Servant by night from al-Masjid al-Haram to al-Masjid al-Aqsa, whose surroundings We have blessed, to show him of Our signs. Indeed, He is the Hearing, the Seeing.  

When we talk about journeys in our tradition, we're not just thinking about getting on a plane or a car. It's more about an inner, spiritual trip. Your body remains stationary, much like when we're sleeping, but our soul – that light, subtle part of us – is out there exploring and connecting with the spiritual world. It’s kind of like dreaming, right? Our body may be still, but in our dreams, our mind can be anywhere, seeing all sorts of things. The Prophet’s journey, however, was far beyond a typical dream; it was a profoundly spiritual experience where his spirit ascended to the highest heavens and witnessed incredible signs from Allah, even though his physical body remained still.

Here's the core idea for us: the real "you" isn't your body. Your body is just like a temporary car that your soul drives around in while you're here on Earth. Your soul is the actual driver, the one experiencing everything.

Now, Prophets and Imams are so spiritually pure, they can see things and understand realities that are hidden from most of us. Their spiritual journeys give us a peek into that ultimate reality, which is precisely where every believer's soul is trying to go.

The Imam of the Time is like a living bridge to that spiritual world, a direct link to the invisible realm (مَلَكُوت). Through his guidance (تَعْلِيم), he helps our souls ascend. It's as if he's holding a map, guiding us to understand the true origins of our soul (أَصْل) and its ultimate destination. Just as the Prophet's soul took an incredible journey in his ascension (مِعْرَاج), our own souls can begin their spiritual climb. How? By focusing on the Imam of the Time and seeking that deep, inner knowledge and recognition (مَعْرِفَة) that he provides. As we're learning right now, our souls are connecting with the spiritual realm. The veils are being lifted before our eyes, enabling us to see beyond the superficial. 

The Experience of Receiving Divine Guidance

Receiving divine revelation, or وَحْي, is a compelling spiritual experience. For Rasul Allah (SWS), this divine communication would often begin with true visions during his sleep. These dreams were a form of the divine message he was to receive. When the revelation came to him while he was awake, he experienced immense gravity, described in the Holy Quran as a "weighty word." He felt like he was being pulled into the earth.

The Enshrouded One (73:5)

‏ ‏إنَّا سَنُلْقى عَلَيْكَ قَوْلًۭا ثَقيلًا ‎  ‎

Translation:

Indeed, We will cast upon you a heavy word.  

This experience was so intense that it would overwhelm his physical being. He would enter a deep, trance-like state and perspire profusely, even on the coldest of days. This demonstrates that the connection between the spiritual world of revelation and the physical world is so potent that it produces tangible, physical effects. Imagine trying to pour the entire ocean into a small cup; the cup would inevitably overflow. In the same way, the Prophet’s physical body was the vessel receiving a vast and powerful message from the spiritual ocean of Divine Knowledge, and the intensity of this influx caused his body to react very strongly.

Some people struggle to comprehend experiences that are far beyond ordinary human understanding. When faced with something completely unfamiliar, a common human reaction is fear or disbelief, leading them to accuse the person of being possessed or unwell. This response highlights the challenges individuals face in grasping a direct manifestation of a higher reality.

In our tradition, while revelation (وحي), in the form of a new religious law or scripture, concluded with Prophet Muhammad (SWS) as the final Prophet, the need for divine guidance for humanity continues. The Imam of the Time continuously receives divine inspiration and support, known as تأييد, to guide the community. This guidance is the "living word" for the present time, serving as the Rope of Allah (حبل الله) that believers are encouraged to hold onto. Just as the Prophet experienced the "weighty word," the Imam of the Time bears the spiritual authority and responsibility of guiding his spiritual children. His guidance illuminates the inner, spiritual meaning (باطن) of the Quran and the faith, making these profound realities relevant and accessible in every time and place. This continuous guidance is the Proof (حجة) of Allah's ever-present mercy and engagement with His creation in every age.

An Imam In Training

The first story about Ibrahim (AS) in the Quran describes his journey to recognize Allah. It is a powerful narrative in the Quran that highlights the use of reason, observation, and innate spiritual disposition (فِطْرَة) to arrive at the truth of تَوْحِيد. It offers us a beautiful lesson in understanding Allah's oneness (تَوْحِيد) by simply looking at the world around us and using our minds. Ibrahim (AS) grew up in a place where people worshipped statues, and even his own father, Azar, was a famous statue maker. But even as a young boy, Ibrahim (AS) knew in his heart that it didn't make sense to pray to things made by humans that couldn't help or hurt anyone. This strong feeling led him to challenge his father, saying:

Cattle (6:74)

‏ ‏ وَإذْ قَالَ إبْرَهيمُ لأَبيه آزَرَ أَتَتَّخذُ أَصْنَامًا آلهَةً ۖ إنّى أَرَىٰكَ وَقَوْمَكَ فى ضَلَلٍۢ مُّبينٍۢ ‎  ‎

Translation:

And [mention, O Muhammad], when Abraham said to his father Azar, "Do you take idols as deities? Indeed, I see you and your people to be in manifest error."  

Seeking the True Allah

Because he was unhappy with what his people believed, Ibrahim (AS) set out to find the one true Allah who truly deserved to be worshipped. Allah guided him on this search by showing him how to use logic, starting with the stars and planets that his people already worshipped. This journey of discovery occurred over just one night and day, revealing to Ibrahim (AS) the immense expanse of the heavens and the earth, which further strengthened his faith.

The Star, the Moon, and the Sun

Cattle (6:76)

‏ ‏فَلَمَّا جَنَّ عَلَيْه ٱلَّيْلُ رَآ كَوْكَبًۭا ۖ قَالَ هَذَا رَبّى ۖ فَلَمَّا أَفَلَ قَالَ لَآ أُحبُّ ٱلْآفلينَ ‎  ‎

Translation:

So when the night covered him [with darkness], he saw a star. He said, "This is my lord." But when it set, he said, "I like not those that disappear."  

As night approached, Ibrahim (AS) saw a star. He pondered whether it could be his lord, but when it disappeared, he realized that a true deity couldn't just vanish. He said, "I like not those that disappear."

Cattle (6:77)

‏ ‏فَلَمَّا رَآ ٱلْقَمَرَ بَازغًۭا قَالَ هَذَا رَبّى ۖ فَلَمَّا أَفَلَ قَالَ لَىٕن لَّمْ يَهْدنى رَبّى لَأَكُونَنَّ منَ ٱلْقَوْم ٱلضَّالّينَ ‎  ‎

And when he saw the moon rising, he said, "This is my lord." But when it set, he said, "Unless my Lord guides me, I will surely be among the people gone astray."  

Next, he watched the moon rise, larger and brighter than the star. He again wondered if it was his true Master. When the moon set, he realized he needed Allah's guidance, understanding that his own reasoning might not suffice without help from the Creator he sought. 

Cattle (6:78)

‏ ‏فَلَمَّا رَآ ٱلشَّمْسَ بَازغَةًۭ قَالَ هَذَا رَبّى هَذَا أَكْبَرُ ۖ فَلَمَّا أَفَلَتْ قَالَ يَقَوْم إنّى بَرىءٌۭ مّمَّا تُشْركُونَ ‎  ‎

Translation:

And when he saw the sun rising, he said, "This is my lord; this is greater." But when it set, he said, "O my people, indeed I am free from what you associate with Allah.  

As dawn arrived, he saw the sun shining brightly and thought, "This is my Master; this is the greatest." However, when the sun also set, he told his people that he had no part in the objects they worshipped alongside Allah. This moment led him to completely reject all forms of polytheism (شِرْك), recognizing that anything that changes or disappears cannot be the ultimate Creator.

The Conclusion of Monotheism

By carefully and logically ruling out all created things as possible gods, Ibrahim (AS) arrived at the pure and simple truth of Allah's oneness (تَوْحِيد). He turned his entire being towards the one and only Creator, who is eternal and absolute. Then, he made his famous and influential declaration, which we still say in our daily prayers today: 

Cattle (6:79)

‏ ‏إنّى وَجَّهْتُ وَجْهىَ للَّذى فَطَرَ ٱلسَّمَوَت وَٱلْأَرْضَ حَنيفًۭا ۖ وَمَا أَنَا۠ منَ ٱلْمُشْركينَ ‎  ‎

Translation:

Indeed, I have turned my face toward He who created the heavens and the earth, inclining toward truth, and I am not of those who associate others with Allah."  

This entire experience shows how Allah fulfilled His promise to reveal to Ibrahim (AS) the spiritual dominion (مَلَكُوت) of reality. 

Cattle (6:75)

Translation:

And thus did We show Abraham the realm of the heavens and the earth that he would be among the certain [in faith]  

By successfully navigating these earthly levels of guidance (مَلَكُوت الْأَرْض), Ibrahim (AS) became worthy of understanding the celestial truths (مَلَكُوت السَّمَاوَات) and reached a state of absolute, unwavering faith.

The Divine Appointment of the Imam

At this point, after completing his spiritual tests, which were essentially steps in his soul's ascent toward the spiritual realms, Ibrahim (AS) was lifted to a special spiritual position by Allah. Allah appointed him as an Imam. Who is Allah here? His own father, Imam Saleh (AS). The Quran tells us about this special appointment: 

The Cow (2:124)

‏ ‏ وَإذ ٱبْتَلَىٰ إبْرَهمَ رَبُّهُ بكَلمَتٍۢ فَأَتَمَّهُنَّ ۖ قَالَ إنّى جَاعلُكَ للنَّاس إمَامًۭا ۖ قَالَ وَمن ذُرّيَّتى ۖ قَالَ لَا يَنَالُ عَهْدى ٱلظَّلمينَ ‎  ‎

Translation:

And [mention, O Muhammad], when Abraham was tried by his Lord with commands and he fulfilled them. [Allah] said, "Indeed, I will make you a leader for the people." [Abraham] said, "And of my descendants?" [Allah] said, "My covenant does not include the wrongdoers."  

What are these كَلِمَتٍۢ? What are these strings of Alphabets? The star, the moon, and the sun. Only after successfully navigating these earthly levels of guidance (مَلَكُوت الْأَرْض), Ibrahim (AS) became worthy of understanding the celestial truths (مَلَكُوت السَّمَاوَات) and reached a state of absolute, unwavering faith. This is when he is appointed the next Imam by his father, Imam Saleh (AS) - قَالَ إِنِّى جَاعِلُكَ لِلنَّاسِ إِمَامًۭا [Allah] said, "Indeed, I will make you a leader for the people." 

قَالَ لَا يَنَالُ عَهْدِى ٱلظَّلِمِينَ

Ibrahim, now Imam Ibrahim (AS), asks, قَالَ وَمِن ذُرِّيَّتِى. What about my progeny? Allah says قَالَ لَا يَنَالُ عَهْدِى ٱلظَّلِمِينَ  My covenant does not include the wrongdoers.

This verse reveals a couple of things:

  1. First, it shows that the Imamate isn't just a position in this world; it's a divine promise. That's why the word عَهْد is used. This is a promise that was made in a world of pure light, before anything else was created. It's a very high rank given to an ethereal soul that is pure from the very beginning. 
  2. Secondly, when Ibrahim (AS) asked if his descendants would also receive this role, Allah's reply made it clear what kind of person can receive this promise. He said:
     قَالَ لَا يَنَالُ عَهْدِي الظَّالِمِينَ - “My covenant does not include the wrongdoers.” 

Here, the term ظَالِم (wrongdoer) in تاویل is a profound word with a deep, hidden meaning that extends beyond someone who sins. It refers to a spiritually dark soul, fundamentally out of tune with Allah's perfect order. The primary manifestation of this darkness, this ظلم, occurs when someone attempts to assume the spiritual position of Imamat that they don't deserve because they are trying to place a dark soul where a soul of light (نُور) should be.

Imagine a special torch designed by a brilliant inventor to shine a unique, pure light. This torch is meant to be passed down through a family. Still, the inventor makes a rule: only those who are specifically chosen by him, and whose internal essence is perfectly pure, can carry and operate this torch effectively.

  • The Torch: This torch is like the Imamate, a divine covenant (عَهْد) from Allah. It carries a unique, pure light (نُور), which is the divine guidance.
  • The Inventor's Rule: When the inventor says, "My covenant does not include the wrongdoers (ظَالِمِينَ)," he means that anyone whose internal essence is flawed, or who tries to steal or grab the torch without being properly designated by the inventor, cannot truly carry its pure light. They might hold the physical torch, but it won't shine properly in their hands. It will eventually sputter and die because the inner essence of the one holding it is not compatible with its pure function. Therefore, the "wrongdoer" here isn't just someone who makes a mistake or sins. It's someone whose very nature or action is fundamentally misaligned with the purpose of the torch – someone who tries to usurp the role without the required inner purity and designation from the inventor.

This process of Imamat, transmitting from father to son, ensures that the divine promise of light and guidance remains unbroken throughout all time. It's not nepotism; it's divine wisdom. 

This entire journey demonstrates how Allah fulfilled His promise to reveal to Ibrahim (AS) the spiritual dominion (مَلَكُوت) of reality. By successfully navigating these earthly levels of guidance (مَلَكُوت الْأَرْض), he became worthy of understanding the celestial truths (مَلَكُوت السَّمَاوَات) and reached a state of absolute, unwavering faith. 

The Conversation With Azar

Let's examine the story of Ibrahim (AS) conversing with his "father" and breaking idols. These are fantastic examples teaching us about the difference between a static, inherited faith and a living, intellectual faith.

The Spiritual Father and the Living Invitation

When the Quran narrates that Ibrahim (AS) is speaking with his "father" (أَب), it is not referring to his biological father, Azar. Think of "father" here as his primary teacher, the elder who raised him in the common religious traditions of their time. We know from the tradition of Rasul Allah (SWS) that all the prophets come from a pure family line of true believers, so it wouldn't make sense for the biological father of Ibrahim (AS) to be an idol worshipper. Azar represents the leader of the established religious system of Nuh at the time.

Similarly, the "idols" he worshipped were not just statues made of wood or stone. Those "idols" are a symbol for rigid ideas and beliefs that people follow without thinking. They are like statues because they're lifeless—they can't answer questions, offer any guidance, or provide spiritual energy. They are upheld by scholars who may hold high ranks but are spiritually closed off to living truths.

So, when Allah granted Ibrahim (AS) special, divine knowledge, he went back to his teacher. He wasn't being rude; he was trying to help him level up. Imagine you learn basic addition from your first-grade teacher. Years later, you become a scientist who understands the whole universe through complex physics. If you go back to your old teacher, you might say, "What you taught me was the perfect start, but I've found a deeper way to see things. Let me show you." That's precisely what Ibrahim (AS) was doing. He was inviting his teacher to move beyond blind copying and to embrace a living faith, guided by fresh knowledge from Allah. 

Mary (19:42)

‏ ‏إذْ قَالَ لأَبيه يَأَبَت لمَ تَعْبُدُ مَا لَا يَسْمَعُ وَلَا يُبْصرُ وَلَا يُغْنى عَنكَ شَيْـًۭٔا ‎  ‎

Translation:

[Mention] when he said to his father, "O my father, why do you worship that which does not hear and does not see and will not benefit you at all?  

This "straight path" (صِرَطًا سَوِيًّا), he mentions, is the timeless path of divine guidance that, in every age, leads a person to recognize the living Imam of his Time. It’s a kind call to leave behind old, rusty ideas and connect with the living knowledge available right now.

The Four Birds

The Quranic story of Ibrahim (AS) asking Allah to show him how He brings life to the dead is a request for a deeper understanding, not because he doubted Allah, but because he wanted to witness spiritual revival directly. He sought to understand how a human soul can be transformed from the "death" of not knowing (ignorance) and being focused solely on worldly things, to the true "life" of spiritual Gnosis (مَعْرِفَة), which is a profound, intuitive understanding of Allah.

The Parable of the Four Birds

Allah's response to Ibrahim (AS)'s question is a living parable for how His divine mission (الدعوة) is established and how souls are awakened:

The Cow (2:260)

‏ ‏وَإذْ قَالَ إبْرَهیمُ رَبّ أَرنى كَيْفَ تُحْى ٱلْمَوْتَىٰ ۖ قَالَ أَوَلَمْ تُؤْمن ۖ قَالَ بَلَىٰ وَلَكن لّيَطْمَئنَّ قَلْبى ۖ قَالَ فَخُذْ أَرْبَعَةًۭ مّنَ ٱلطَّيْر فَصُرْهُنَّ إلَيْكَ ثُمَّ ٱجْعَلْ عَلَىٰ كُلّ جَبَلٍۢ مّنْهُنَّ جُزْءًۭا ثُمَّ ٱدْعُهُنَّ يَأْتينَكَ سَعْيًۭا وَٱعْلَمْ أَنَّ ٱللَّهَ عَزيزٌ حَكيمٌۭ ‎  ‎

Translation:

And [mention] when Abraham said, "My Lord, show me how You give life to the dead." [Allah] said, "Have you not believed?" He said, "Yes, but [I ask] only that my heart may be satisfied." [Allah] said, "Take four birds and commit them to yourself. Then [after slaughtering them] put on each hill a portion of them; then call them - they will come [flying] to you in haste. And know that Allah is Exalted in Might and Wise."  

Ibrahim (AS) asks Allah, رَبِّ أَرِنِى كَيْفَ تُحْىِ ٱلْمَوْتَىٰ Master, show me how you bring dead souls back to life? Allah asks: قَالَ أَوَلَمْ تُؤْمِن. Do you not believe? Ibrahim (AS) clarifies his motives by saying: قَالَ بَلَىٰ وَلَكِن لِّيَطْمَئِنَّ قَلْبِى, "Yes, but [I ask] only so that my heart becomes reassured." He had the faith that comes from knowledge (عِلْمُ الْيَقِين), but he sought the certainty of direct vision (عَيْنُ الْيَقِين). It's like knowing from a blueprint that an engine should work versus seeing and hearing it roar to life. He wanted to witness the beautiful system through which Allah grants the soul true life.

In response, Allah commanded him to take four birds, tame them, and make them incline towards him. 

  • Birds symbolize pure souls, as they can ascend towards the heavens. These four birds represent four pure human souls, who are among the highest ranks of the spiritual hierarchy (حُدُود الدِّين) and are ready to receive divine knowledge directly from the Imam. They are like his chief deputies or Proofs (ابواب). The command to tame these birds symbolizes the intense spiritual training (تَرْبِيَة) and discipline that the Imam provides to his deputies. He shares his knowledge with them until their hearts and minds are perfectly aligned with his own, becoming perfect reflections of his wisdom.
  • Ibrahim (AS) was then told to place each bird on a separate "mountain" (جَبَل). A mountain here is not just a physical peak; it's a symbol of a significant region or a community of faith (جَزِيرَة) in the world. Placing a trained deputy on a "mountain" means appointing these four حجۃ as the head of the spiritual mission in that land, making them a firm and elevated source of guidance for its people.
  • When Ibrahim (AS) calls to them, they return to him instantly, "in haste." This shows the absolute power and unity of the Imam's spiritual call. No matter how far away they are physically, his trained deputies are in perfect, immediate spiritual connection with him. His call is a spiritual reality that goes beyond space and time, and their response is immediate and specific because they are extensions of his own spiritual will.

Practical Example: 

Think about it this way. Imagine a highly skilled CEO with a clear vision for their company. They don't run every single branch themselves. Instead, they choose four highly trusted and capable regional managers (the four birds) and train them personally and extensively (taming the birds) until these managers fully understand the CEO's vision and methods. Then, the CEO assigns each manager to a different, important region (mountain). When the CEO makes a crucial decision or issues a strategic directive, these managers instantly understand and implement it, even across vast distances, because they are so perfectly aligned with the CEO's will and vision. 

This parable is Allah’s answer to Ibrahim (AS), demonstrating how He gives life to the "dead" souls: He does so through His chosen guide, the Imam, who trains and sends out his representatives throughout the world. These deputies, carrying the Imam’s call, awaken the souls of humanity, reviving them with the eternal life of knowledge and faith. This is the living, breathing proof of Allah’s system for the salvation of humankind.

Breaking the idols

The well-known story of Ibrahim (AS) illustrates how, during a festival when people were away celebrating, Ibrahim (AS) seized a brave opportunity to make a significant statement. When Ibrahim (AS) was sure that everyone had left town, he entered the temple and caught sight of the various idols made of wood and stone that were worshiped by the townsfolk. He also noticed that food had been placed before the idols as offerings by the people. He challenges them as the Quran states:

Those Who Set the Ranks (37:92)

‏ ‏مَا لَكُمْ لَا تَنطقُونَ ‎  ‎

Translation:

What is [wrong] with you that you do not speak? "  

He then broke all the idols except the largest one, hanging the axe on its shoulder to demonstrate its helplessness. His goal was for the people to realize that their idols couldn’t be true gods because they were powerless. When they returned and saw the destroyed statues, they became angry and suspected Ibrahim (AS), bringing him before the assembly to determine who was responsible. Ibrahim (AS) confidently declared, 

The Prophets (21:63)

‏ ‏قَالَ بَلْ فَعَلَهُ كَبيرُهُمْ هَذَا فَسْـَٔلُوهُمْ إن كَانُوا۟ يَنطقُونَ ‎  ‎

Translation:

He said, "Rather, this - the largest of them - did it, so ask them, if they should [be able to] speak."

He challenged their logic, questioning how a large, immovable statue could break smaller ones, and if it couldn’t defend itself, how it could be considered a god. The crowd was left speechless as they recognized their idols' helplessness and understood they had been wrong to worship them. However, their pride and stubbornness soon led them to plan severe punishment for Ibrahim (AS). 

ESOTERICS

The Fire of Persecution

The "blazing fire" is a symbol for something far more terrifying than literal flames; it represents public opinion that has been weaponized by falsehood. It is the fire of mass hysteria, collective rage, and a society's complete and passionate commitment to a lie. At its core, the fire was a trial of persecution (فِتْنَة). It began with a campaign of lies and slander designed to destroy and tarnish the character of Allah’s guide. The goal was strategic: to ensure that when the final attack comes, no one will rush to his defense, because the fire of rumors has already tarnished his credibility. Everyone will have been convinced that he is a dangerous person.

Think of how a rumor spreads like wildfire through a school or a community. It starts with a small spark but, fanned by gossip and malice, it quickly grows into a raging blaze that consumes friendships, trust, and reputations. This was precisely the tactic used against Ibrahim (AS).

From Rumor to Roaring Inferno

This small fire of slander is then deliberately fanned into a "blazing fire" by the powers that be. The irrational, emotional anger of the crowd is channeled into a single, roaring force. This is the fire of mob mentality, where the heat of blind passion replaces all reason.

The very scale of the fire in the story symbolizes this escalation. It wasn't a small, hidden fire; it was a massive public spectacle. The purpose was to make the destruction of the truth a terrifying lesson for all to see. It was a show trial on a grand scale, meant to intimidate anyone who might dare to think differently and challenge the authority of the establishment. The message was clear: "This is what happens to those who oppose us."

This pattern of creating a "fire" to consume the truth is not unique to Ibrahim (AS). It is the recurring test (فِتْنَة) that Allah's guides have faced throughout history. We see a perfect example with Rasul Allah (SWS). When he began preaching the message of one Allah, the leaders of Quraysh didn't engage him with logic. They launched a massive smear campaign. They tried to "burn" his message by calling him a sorcerer, a madman, and a liar. They claimed he was only seeking power and wealth. They were trying to create a fire of hatred and suspicion around him so that no one would listen to his actual words.
